# Passenger Demographics, Experience & Outcomes Dashboard

## Executive Summary

This project is a Power BI infographic-style dashboard built to analyse passenger demographics, sentiment, and service experience across the 2021–2022 survey period.

The report brings together **129,880 survey records** to support demographic profiling, cohort comparison, year-over-year reporting, service indicator tracking, and recommendation-focused insights. Key findings included a **46-point improvement in Net Promoter Score (NPS)** from **1 in 2021** to **47 in 2022**, alongside broader improvement across service experience measures.

The dashboard also highlighted weaker areas requiring closer monitoring, particularly **online booking**, **in-flight Wi-Fi**, and **delay-related experience**, and translated these findings into clear recommendations for ongoing reporting and service improvement.

---

## Business Problem

Passenger survey data often includes multiple dimensions of information, such as:

- respondent demographics
- recommendation behaviour
- service experience ratings
- year-over-year changes in satisfaction

When these indicators are spread across different fields, it becomes difficult to clearly understand:

- who the main respondent groups are
- whether sentiment is improving over time
- which service areas are performing well or poorly
- what issues should be prioritised for monitoring and improvement

The goal of this project was to transform raw survey data into a structured Power BI report that supports **cohort analysis, trend monitoring, indicator reporting, and accessible communication for non-technical audiences**.

## Methodology

### 1. Data Preparation and Modelling
- Built a structured reporting model in Power BI to support analysis across the **2021–2022** survey period.
- Developed measures for:
  - response count
  - promoter / detractor segmentation
  - Net Promoter Score (NPS)
  - year-over-year (YOY) change
  - average journey score
- Used **Power Query** to restructure service rating fields into a reporting-ready indicator model for easier comparison across the passenger journey.

### 2. Demographic & Cohort Analysis
- Analysed respondent patterns by:
  - customer type
  - class
  - type of travel
  - gender
  - age group
- Compared **2022 responses (65,326)** and **2021 responses (64,554)** to assess respondent stability over time.
- Identified that **82% of 2022 respondents were returning passengers**, supporting a stable cohort for year-over-year comparison.

### 3. Sentiment & NPS Analysis
- Calculated **Net Promoter Score (NPS)** to assess respondent sentiment.
- Compared NPS across age groups and years.
- Built a year-over-year NPS view showing a **+46 point improvement**, with NPS increasing from **1 in 2021** to **47 in 2022**.

### 4. Service Experience Indicator Analysis
- Tracked score distribution across key service indicators, including:
  - departure and arrival time convenience
  - ease of online booking
  - check-in service
  - online boarding
  - gate location
  - on-board service
  - seat comfort
  - leg room service
  - cleanliness
  - food and drink
  - in-flight service
  - in-flight Wi-Fi service
  - in-flight entertainment
  - baggage handling
- Analysed year-over-year score change by category and service attribute.
- Explored the relationship between **departure delay** and **average passenger score**.

### 5. Reporting & Data Storytelling
- Organised the report into five sections:
  - Key Findings
  - Demographic & Cohort Profile
  - Sentiment & Advocacy Indicators
  - Service Experience Indicators
  - Recommendations
- Presented findings in an **infographic-style dashboard** designed for clear interpretation by non-technical audiences.
